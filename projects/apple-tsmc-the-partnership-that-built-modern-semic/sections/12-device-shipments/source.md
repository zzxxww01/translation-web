The device base is massive but mature. Apple now ships over 600 million devices annually across all categories. But growth rates tell a different story: the 20%+ annual growth of 2020-2021 has compressed to around 3% through the forecast period. The portfolio is collectively plateauing.

iPhone volumes peaked nearly a decade ago and have oscillated in a narrow band ever since. The forecast through 2030 shows low-single-digit growth, barely keeping pace with population. iPhone drives the majority of unit volumes but contributes almost nothing to incremental device growth.

Wearables carried growth, then stalled. AirPods was the fastest-ramping product in Apple’s history, reaching meaningful scale within four years of launch. Watch followed a similar trajectory. Both categories have since plateaued, with growth rates compressing to low-single-digits by 2025.

New categories are not moving the needle. Vision Pro shipments through 2030 remain a rounding error relative to the 600M+ device base. Its contribution is silicon intensity, primarily. The C-series chips powering spatial computing consume wafer capacity disproportionate to device shipments.

Server appears in the forecast. A new “Server” line item emerges in 2024 and grows through 2030. Volumes are small, but the category’s existence signals Apple’s datacenter silicon ambitions (Baltra) are more than rumors.

The 2025-2030 growth outlook is muted. Total device shipment growth averages around 3% annually through the forecast, down from double-digits earlier in the decade. Apple’s wafer demand growth will increasingly decouple from device unit growth, driven by silicon intensity per device rather than more devices shipped.
