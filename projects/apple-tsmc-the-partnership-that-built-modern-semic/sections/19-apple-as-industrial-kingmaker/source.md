Apple as the Engine of Global Semiconductor Capex; Apple as Industrial Kingmaker

Apple’s demand has catalyzed hundreds of billions in capital expenditure across the semiconductor ecosystem. The company ships over 600 million devices annually containing thousands of individual components, making it the single largest driver of precision manufacturing investment globally.

The Companies Apple Built

Foxconn: Revenue grew from approximately $40B in 2006 to $208B in 2024 (TWD 6.86 trillion). The company now operates 230+ facilities across 24 countries with 900,000 peak-season employees.

ASML: Apple has never purchased directly from ASML, yet Apple may be the primary reason ASML exists in its current form. Apple’s leading-edge chip requirements drive TSMC’s demand for EUV lithography. TSMC is ASML’s largest customer. ASML’s market cap grew from approximately €15B in 2010 to €380B+ in 2024, a 25x increase during the Apple-TSMC era.

TSMC: Apple volumes consume the majority of TSMC’s leading-edge capacity. TSMC’s capital expenditure has increased fivefold since 2015, reaching $29.8B in 2024 and targeting $40-42B in 2025. The company is building nine new facilities in 2025 alone.

Apple’s $3 trillion market cap has driven $300B+ in supplier capital expenditure over the past decade. Multiple companies count Apple as 40-75% of their revenue. The invisible hand of Apple’s volume requirements reaches through three supply chain layers to build companies Apple will never directly touch.
