Apple pioneered TSMC’s advanced packaging business. InFO launched with the A10 in 2016, and Apple scaled it from $1.8B in 2018 to $3.8B in 2025. CoWoS was a rounding error at $118M in 2018. By 2025, it reached $9.6B. The crossover happened in 2022 when AI demand exploded. Apple created TSMC’s advanced packaging business with InFO. Nvidia’s CoWoS demand now drives it.

Apple accounted for 78% of TSMC’s non-wafer revenue in 2020. That was peak dominance. InFO was scaling for A-series chips, and CoWoS had few customers. By 2024, Apple’s share dropped to 36%. By 2025, it falls further to 30%. Apple’s absolute packaging revenue continues growing. But AI customers, led by Nvidia, are scaling CoWoS faster.
