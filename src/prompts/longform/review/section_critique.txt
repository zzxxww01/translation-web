## Review Priorities
1. Accuracy first: mistranslations, omissions, broken logic, lost conditions, wrong comparisons, wrong time references.
2. Preserve analyst force: do not let strong judgments become bland description.
3. Terminology and first-mention strategy: consistent, helpful, and not over-annotated.
4. Remove translationese — check for these patterns:
   - Abstract noun shells: "存在……" "进行……" "实现……的提升"
   - Late main clause: reader must reach the second half to know the point
   - Trailing English post-modifiers dragged to sentence end
   - Tone inflation: translation stronger than the original judgment
   - Over-splitting: dense but readable sentences broken into fragments
   - Multi-layer modifier stacking: "越来越高度依赖", "可能成为导致……的潜在原因"
   - "通过X来Y从而Z" triple causation chains — should be split into separate sentences
   - English prepositional phrase calques: "对软件可见", "对性能敏感" without Chinese rewriting
   - Sentences over 50 Chinese characters with 2+ nested clauses that need splitting
5. Keep dense passages readable without dropping information. Flag paragraphs stuffing 5+ independent conclusions into a single block — suggest splitting into sub-paragraphs or bullet lists.
6. Check paragraph-to-paragraph flow inside the section.
7. Degree fidelity: check that adjectives, adverbs, and verbs in the translation carry the same intensity as the source. Flag cases where the translation inflates (e.g., "interesting" → "极具挑战") or deflates (e.g., "urge" → "呼吁") the original.
8. Abbreviation hygiene: flag triple annotations like "中文全称 (ENG)（English Full Name (ENG)）" or repeated full expansions of abbreviations already defined earlier in the text.
9. List structure preservation: flag cases where source numbered/bulleted lists were collapsed into a single paragraph or joined by semicolons. Also flag inline numbering (e.g., "1. xxx; 2. xxx") that should be rendered as a proper list.
10. Domain terminology precision: flag CUDA/GPU/semiconductor term mistranslations. Common errors include kernel≠算子 (算子 is operator), latent≠潜在的 (should be 潜变量), floorplan≠布局 (should be 版图), singleton≠零散的.
11. Causal chain accuracy: check if because/since/so/therefore chains in the translation correctly reflect the source's causation direction. Flag reversed causation, missing intermediate steps, or "从而" bridging unrelated clauses.
12. CJK-Latin spacing: flag missing spaces between Chinese characters and adjacent English letters or digits (e.g., "NCU性能" should be "NCU 性能").
13. Chinese-native expression density: flag wordy direct translations where a 四字格, idiom, or concise Chinese expression would be more natural. Examples: "面临断崖式下跌" → "一路陡降"; "随着时间的推移" as filler; "势必将" (semantic overlap). Also flag weak trailing words ("一样", "一般") at the end of simile/metaphor sentences.
14. Metaphor coherence: flag mixed metaphors (two incompatible image directions in one sentence, e.g., "无底洞" + "狂潮") and culturally opaque references that lack translator notes for Chinese readers (e.g., Monaco Grand Prix rental analogy, NPR without explanation).
15. Cross-domain terminology precision: flag terms translated with wrong-domain conventions. Common errors: "出租率" for utilization rate (cloud computing), "代币" for token (AI context), "财务建模" for financial modeling (investment context), "算力" for capacity, "资产折旧年限" for useful economic life.
16. Intensity matching: flag cases where a vivid original expression was flattened to a bland academic word. Examples: "frozen" → "搁置" should be "冻结"; "barometer" → "指标" should be "晴雨表"; "convinced" with overconfidence nuance → "认定" should be "笃定".
17. Weak trailing words: specifically flag unnecessary "一样", "一般", "之类的" at the end of simile or metaphor sentences that dilute impact.
18. Formatting conventions: flag comma used between parallel items where 顿号（、）is correct; Arabic numerals for approximate time spans that should use Chinese numerals (e.g., "6 月到 7 月" → "六七月份"); independent arguments crammed into one paragraph that should be split.
